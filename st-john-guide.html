St. John Travel Guide

The most pristine and protected island in the US Virgin Islands

St. John is widely considered the crown jewel of the US Virgin Islands. More than two-thirds of the island is protected as Virgin Islands National Park, giving it a quiet, natural beauty that feels worlds away from busier Caribbean destinations.

Best Beaches on St. John

Trunk Bay is the most famous, with its iconic underwater snorkel trail. Coral Bay offers a more local, laid-back vibe. Hawksnest Bay and Cinnamon Bay are perfect for relaxed beach days with excellent swimming.

Snorkeling & Marine Life

Waterlemon Cay and other reefs around St. John are among the best places in the Caribbean to snorkel with sea turtles. A good mask and fins make a noticeable difference in comfort and visibility.

Practical Gear for Boat & Beach Days

Many visitors spend full days on boats or exploring remote coves. A waterproof dry bag is one of the most useful items you can bring — it keeps phones, wallets, and towels safe even when waves splash aboard.

Getting Around & Staying

Most people rent a jeep (highly recommended). Ferries from St. Thomas arrive at Cruz Bay. Villa rentals are popular here, many with stunning hilltop views overlooking the bays.
