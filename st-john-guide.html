St. John Travel Guide

The most pristine and protected island in the US Virgin Islands

If you want turquoise water, powdery beaches, and a genuine sense of nature, St. John is the crown jewel of the USVI. Over 60% of the island is protected as Virgin Islands National Park, giving it a relaxed, unspoiled character that sets it apart from its busier neighbors.

Best Beaches on St. John

  • Trunk Bay – Famous for its underwater snorkel trail. One of the most photographed beaches in the Caribbean.
  • Cinnamon Bay – Long, wide beach with calm waters and good facilities.
  • Hawksnest Bay – Quiet and beautiful, great for swimming and picnics.
  • Waterlemon Cay – World-class snorkeling with frequent sea turtle sightings.

Snorkeling & Water Activities

St. John’s underwater world is exceptional. For the best experience, proper equipment makes a noticeable difference — especially a good mask and fins that fit well and a dry snorkel. Many visitors rent gear, but bringing your own favorite set is often more comfortable.

Practical Tips for a Better Trip

Many visitors spend full days on boats or at remote beaches. A waterproof dry bag is one of the most useful items you can bring — it keeps phones, wallets, towels, and snacks safe and dry even if the boat gets splashed or you go swimming.

Getting Around & Staying

Most people rent a jeep or take taxis. Ferries from St. Thomas arrive at Cruz Bay. Villa rentals are very popular here — many are tucked into the hills with stunning views.
