St. John Travel Guide

The most pristine and protected island in the US Virgin Islands

St. John is widely considered the crown jewel of the US Virgin Islands. More than two-thirds of the island is protected as Virgin Islands National Park, giving it a quiet, unspoiled beauty that is increasingly rare in the Caribbean.

Best Beaches on St. John

Trunk Bay is the most famous, known for its underwater snorkel trail and powdery white sand. It can get crowded midday, so arriving early or late is recommended. Cinnamon Bay offers a long, wide beach with good facilities and is popular with families. Hawksnest Bay is quieter and excellent for swimming. Waterlemon Cay is one of the best spots in the Caribbean for snorkeling with sea turtles.

Snorkeling & Marine Life

St. John’s reefs are exceptionally healthy. Waterlemon Cay is legendary for frequent green sea turtle sightings. A good-fitting mask and fins make a significant difference in comfort and enjoyment, especially if you plan to spend hours in the water.

Practical Gear for Boat & Beach Days

Many visitors spend full days on boats or exploring remote coves. A waterproof dry bag is one of the most useful items you can bring — it keeps phones, wallets, towels, and snacks safe even when waves splash aboard.

Hiking & Nature

The Reef Bay Trail leads to ancient petroglyphs and a beautiful waterfall. Ram Head offers dramatic views. Bordeaux Mountain is the highest point on the island. Bring water, good shoes, and insect repellent for longer hikes.

Where to Eat

Skinny Legs in Coral Bay is a local favorite known for excellent burgers and a laid-back atmosphere. The Longboard in Cruz Bay offers great sushi and fresh fish. For a more upscale meal, try The Terrace at The Westin.

Getting Around

Renting a jeep is highly recommended. Taxis are available but can add up quickly. Ferries from St. Thomas arrive at Cruz Bay.
