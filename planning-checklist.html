Virgin Islands Vacation Planning Guide & Checklist

The most practical, detailed resource for planning your trip to the US & British Virgin Islands

Best Time to Visit the Virgin Islands

The USVI and BVI have a tropical climate with two main seasons:

Peak Season (December – April)

Sunny, drier weather with lower humidity and fewer mosquitoes. This is the most popular time with the best sailing conditions. Expect higher prices and more crowds, especially around Christmas and New Year.

Off-Season (May – November)

Significantly lower rates on villas and hotels. You'll see more afternoon showers, but many days are still beautiful. Hurricane season peaks in August–October — many experienced travelers still visit during this time and get great deals.

USVI vs BVI – Which Should You Choose?

The U.S. Virgin Islands (St. Thomas, St. John, St. Croix) are easier for U.S. citizens — no passport is required if flying directly from the U.S. mainland. The British Virgin Islands feel more exclusive and are a sailor’s paradise, but require a passport and have fewer large resorts.

Many people do a split trip: fly into St. Thomas, spend time on St. John, then take the ferry to the BVI.

What to Pack – Comprehensive Checklist

Sun & Reef Protection

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+ broad spectrum) – required by law in the USVI
  • Wide-brim sun hat with UPF 50+ protection
  • Polarized sunglasses
  • Light rash guard or long-sleeve swim shirt for extended snorkeling

Snorkeling & Water Gear

  • Quality snorkel mask, dry snorkel, and fins
  • Waterproof dry bag (10L or larger)
  • Water shoes or reef-safe sandals (many beaches have rocky entries)
  • Underwater phone case or action camera (optional but highly recommended)

Clothing & Daily Essentials

  • Lightweight, quick-dry clothing and swimwear
  • Light rain jacket or compact poncho
  • Insect repellent (DEET-free preferred for evenings)
  • Reusable water bottle and insulated beach bag with cooler compartment

Money-Saving & Practical Tips

Restaurants in the Virgin Islands can be expensive. Many experienced travelers buy groceries upon arrival and cook at their villa. Most grocery stores do not provide bags, so bring reusable ones. Renting a jeep is highly recommended on St. John and St. Croix for maximum flexibility.
