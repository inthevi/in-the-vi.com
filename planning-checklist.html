Virgin Islands Vacation Planning Guide & Checklist

Everything you need to plan a successful, stress-free trip to the US & British Virgin Islands

Best Time to Visit the Virgin Islands

The USVI and BVI have two distinct seasons. December through April is the peak dry season — expect sunny days, lower humidity, and fewer mosquitoes. This is the most popular period, with higher accommodation and flight prices.

May to November is the off-season ("green season"). You'll find significantly lower rates on villas and hotels, but expect more frequent afternoon showers and the risk of tropical storms or hurricanes (peak risk August–October).

USVI vs BVI – Which Should You Choose?

U.S. citizens can travel to the US Virgin Islands without a passport (if arriving by air from the U.S. mainland). The BVI requires a passport and feels more exclusive and sailing-oriented. Many visitors do both islands on one trip using ferries.

What to Pack – Comprehensive Checklist

Sun & Reef Protection (Essential)

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+ broad spectrum) – required by law in the USVI
  • Wide-brim sun hat with UPF protection
  • Polarized sunglasses

Snorkeling & Water Gear

  • Quality snorkel mask, snorkel, and fins
  • Waterproof dry bag (10L+) for boat days
  • Water shoes or reef-safe sandals for rocky beaches

Clothing & Daily Essentials

  • Lightweight, quick-dry clothing
  • Light rain jacket or poncho (for quick showers)
  • Insect repellent (especially for evenings)
  • Reusable water bottle

Practical Tips from Experienced Travelers

Many seasoned visitors buy groceries upon arrival and cook at their villa to save money — restaurants can be quite expensive. Most grocery stores do not provide bags, so bring reusable ones. Renting a jeep is highly recommended on St. John and St. Croix for flexibility.
