Virgin Islands Vacation Planning Guide & Checklist

Everything you need to plan a successful trip to the US & British Virgin Islands

Best Time to Visit the Virgin Islands

The USVI and BVI have a tropical climate with two main seasons. December through April is the peak "dry season" — expect sunny days, lower humidity, and fewer mosquitoes. This is the most popular time, with higher prices and more crowds.

May to November is the "green season" (off-season). Rates drop significantly on villas and hotels, but you’ll have a higher chance of afternoon showers and the hurricane season runs June to November (peak in August–October).

Choosing Between USVI and BVI

The US Virgin Islands (St. Thomas, St. John, St. Croix) are easier for U.S. citizens (no passport needed for direct travel from the mainland). The British Virgin Islands (Tortola, Virgin Gorda, Anegada, Jost Van Dyke) feel more exclusive and sailing-oriented but require a passport.

What to Pack – Comprehensive Checklist

Essentials

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (required by law in the USVI – regular sunscreen damages coral)
  • Snorkel mask, snorkel, and fins (highly recommended for St. John and St. Croix)
  • Waterproof dry bag or waterproof phone pouch
  • Lightweight, quick-dry clothing and a wide-brim sun hat
  • Water shoes or reef-safe water sandals for rocky beaches
  • Insect repellent (especially for evenings and rainforest hikes)
  • Reusable water bottle and insulated beach bag with cooler compartment
  • Power adapter and surge protector for villa stays

Money & Practical Tips

Many seasoned travelers buy groceries upon arrival and cook at their villa to save money, as restaurants can be expensive. Most grocery stores in the USVI do not provide bags, so bring reusable ones. Renting a jeep is highly recommended on St. John and St. Croix for flexibility.
