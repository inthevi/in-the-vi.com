Virgin Islands Vacation Planning Guide & Checklist

The most practical and detailed resource for planning your trip to the US & British Virgin Islands

Best Time to Visit the Virgin Islands

The USVI and BVI enjoy a tropical climate with two main seasons:

Peak Dry Season (December – April)

This is the most popular time with sunny days, lower humidity, and minimal rain. Expect higher prices and more crowds, especially around Christmas, New Year, and Easter. Sailing conditions are generally excellent.

Off-Season / Green Season (May – November)

Rates on villas and hotels drop significantly. You’ll see more afternoon showers, but many days are still beautiful. Hurricane season runs June to November, with the highest risk in August–October. Many experienced travelers visit during this time and get great deals.

US Virgin Islands vs British Virgin Islands

Choosing between the USVI and BVI (or combining both) is one of the most important decisions when planning your trip.

  • USVI (St. Thomas, St. John, St. Croix): Easier for U.S. citizens (no passport required for direct flights from the mainland). More developed infrastructure, larger resorts, and better shopping on St. Thomas.
  • BVI (Tortola, Virgin Gorda, Anegada, Jost Van Dyke): Feels more exclusive and sailing-oriented. Requires a passport. Known for world-class sailing, quieter beaches, and a more upscale, private-island vibe.

Many visitors do a split trip: fly into St. Thomas, spend time on St. John, then take the ferry to the BVI.

What to Pack – Comprehensive Checklist

Sun & Reef Protection (Non-Negotiable)

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+ broad spectrum) – required by law in the USVI
  • Wide-brim sun hat with UPF 50+ protection
  • Polarized sunglasses
  • Light rash guard or long-sleeve swim shirt for extended snorkeling

Snorkeling & Water Activities

  • High-quality snorkel mask, dry snorkel, and fins
  • Waterproof dry bag (10L or larger)
  • Water shoes or reef-safe sandals (many beaches have rocky entries)
  • Underwater phone case or GoPro (optional but highly recommended)

Clothing & Daily Essentials

  • Lightweight, quick-dry clothing and multiple swimsuits
  • Light rain jacket or compact poncho
  • Insect repellent (especially for evenings and rainforest areas)
  • Reusable water bottle and insulated beach bag with cooler compartment

Money-Saving & Practical Tips

Restaurants can be expensive in the Virgin Islands. Many experienced travelers buy groceries upon arrival and cook at their villa. Most grocery stores do not provide bags, so bring reusable ones. Renting a jeep is highly recommended on St. John and St. Croix for maximum flexibility.
