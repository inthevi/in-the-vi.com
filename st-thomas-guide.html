St. Thomas Travel Guide

The vibrant heart and main hub of the US Virgin Islands

St. Thomas is the busiest and most developed of the US Virgin Islands. It serves as the main gateway for most visitors, with excellent shopping in Charlotte Amalie, beautiful beaches, and easy ferry access to St. John and the BVI.

Best Beaches on St. Thomas

Magens Bay is frequently ranked among the most beautiful beaches in the world, with calm, turquoise water and soft white sand. It can get crowded when cruise ships are in port, so arriving early or late is recommended. Coki Beach is excellent for snorkeling right off the shore, often with sea lions. Sapphire Beach and Lindquist Beach offer calmer, less crowded alternatives.

Sun Protection

The Caribbean sun is intense. Reef-safe sunscreen is required by law throughout the USVI to protect the coral reefs. A quality broad-spectrum option is essential for all-day beach or boat activities.

Practical Gear for Beach & Villa Days

Many visitors buy groceries to cook at their villa rather than eating out every meal. An insulated beach bag with a cooler compartment is very useful for keeping drinks cold and food fresh during day trips.

Charlotte Amalie & Shopping

The capital offers excellent duty-free shopping. Many visitors combine shopping with a visit to the historic district and Havensight Mall when cruise ships are in port. For a more relaxed experience, visit on days with fewer ships.

Getting Around St. Thomas

Taxis are plentiful but can add up quickly. Private transfers from the airport to your villa are often worth it for comfort, especially if you have a lot of luggage. Renting a car is convenient if you plan to explore beyond the main tourist areas.
